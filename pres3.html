Ramadan: A Month of Reflection and Community

The Significance of Ramadan

Ramadan is a month celebrated by Muslim people around the world. During this month, people do not eat or drink from sunrise to sunset. They do this to think about the poor, the sick, and the lonely people in the world. They also do this to remember how blessed they are to have what they need in their lives.

Breaking the Fast

When the sun sets, families pray and break their fast together with dates, salad, soup, and a delicious family meal. Dessert is also served, and everyone enjoys water, juices, and milk.

Ramadan: A Special Time

What Muslims Do During Ramadan

Muslims fast during Ramadan. This means they don't eat or drink from sunrise to sunset. It helps them feel more thankful and remember people who don't have enough food or water.

Why Ramadan Is Important

Ramadan is a time for Muslims to feel closer to God. They read the Quran more and do good things for others. It's a time to forgive and think about how they can be better people.

Ending the Fast

When the sun goes down, it's time to eat! Families gather to eat dates, soup, and salad. They drink water, juice, or milk. It's a special time for families to be together and pray.

Charity During Ramadan

During Ramadan, Muslims give to charity. They help people who need food, clothes, or money. It's a way to share their blessings and make the world a better place.

The Celebration of Eid al-Fitr

After Ramadan, Muslims celebrate Eid al-Fitr. It's a big holiday! Families pray together, eat delicious food, and give gifts to each other. It's a time of happiness and thanks.
